About Me

I am a Research and Project Coordinator at Pontificia Universidad Católica and Universidad de Chile, where I coordinate a research team conducting an experimental impact evaluation on rent subsidy utilization and public officials’ performance, led by Professors Pablo Celhay and Javiera Selman and funded by Chile’s Budget Office (DIPRES). I hold a Master’s Degree in Public Policy (2020–2021) and a B.S. in Civil Engineering of Industry (2014–2019), both from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile. My work explores how data and evidence can improve decision-making processes in the public sector.

Research Interests

My work explores how the provision of information and technology can strengthen public management and enhance students’ educational outcomes, with a focus on education policy, social programs, and human capital development.

Selected Projects

  • Assistance for Public Officials – Project on improving communication between local officials and housing subsidy beneficiaries.
  • Education Data Analysis – Study on factors influencing standardized test performance in Chile.
